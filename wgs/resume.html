Resume - Mark Hoel

My name is Mark Hoel and I am a recently retired computer science, design and geography teacher from Toronto. I enjoy working in interactive roles where I can be creative, and work with data about real places and environments.

I started my career at the College of Geographic Sciences in Nova Scotia, Canada's largest geospatial learning environment. After graduation, myself and number of my classmates were hired by ESRI in Redlands, California, the world's largest GIS software vendor. Eventually I got married and moved back to Canada to earn my Masters in Environmental Studies and began working as a programmer for Akanda Innovation, a ground-breaking start-up eventually aquired by the Fortune 500 company, Tyler Technologies. Finally, I moved into education as the head of Geography at Upper Canada College, one of Canada's oldest and most presitious all boys private schools.

During my professional career, I have taken time out to complete a masters and start a PhD, as well as take a year off to travel the world with my family.

For more information, please take a look at my two page resume (pdf)

Summary

I have been lucky to have had a number of high quality experiences in my life and work with organizations that are recognized leaders in their fields. At the same time, I have been able to indulge my passions for geography, research, data and data visualization as well as GIS and programming.
